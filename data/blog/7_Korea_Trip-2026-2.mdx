After five days exploring Seoul, we flew south to Jeju Island - South Korea's subtropical volcanic gem.

Jeju felt like a completely different country from Seoul: volcanic rock everywhere, ocean views at every turn, and a pace of life that immediately slowed us down. Five days on the island, followed by a quick hop to Busan, made up the second leg of our trip.

## First Impressions: Jeju City

After landing at Jeju Airport on Thursday afternoon, we took a taxi to Hotel Whistlelark Jeju in Jeju City and checked in from 15:00. With the afternoon still ahead of us, we headed out to explore. Our first stop was Yongduam Rock - the Dragon Head Rock - a naturally formed lava rock on the coast, shaped like a dragon rising from the sea. It was striking against the ocean backdrop, waves crashing around the dark volcanic stone. Standing there, Jeju already felt like a completely different world from Seoul.

## Jeju City on Foot

Friday was dedicated to exploring Jeju City itself. We started on Chilseong-ro, one of Jeju's main shopping streets, before wandering into Dongmun Traditional Market. The market was alive with colour and noise - stalls selling fresh seafood, Jeju tangerines, hallabong juice, and local street food. It had the energy of Myeongdong's markets but with a distinctly island flavour.

From there, we visited the Jeju Museum of Folk & Natural History, which gave us context for the island's unique culture - its volcanic geology, the famous haenyeo (women divers), and how island life developed differently from the mainland. Samseonghyeol followed, a sacred site where Jeju's three founding ancestors are said to have emerged from the ground. The ancient grounds, with their old trees and quiet atmosphere, offered a mythological layer to the island's story.

We ended the day at Gwandeokjeong Hall, one of Jeju's oldest buildings, sitting in the square and watching the city go by. Jeju City had a relaxed charm - not trying to impress, just being itself.

## West and South Coast Day Tour

Saturday brought a full-day tour of Jeju's west and south coasts. We were picked up at 08:25 and spent the day covering ground we couldn't easily reach on our own.

The day started with views of Mt. Halla, Jeju's dormant volcano and South Korea's highest peak, its summit often hidden in cloud. Camellia Hill Botanical Garden was unexpectedly beautiful - winding paths through camellia forests, sculptures, and garden rooms that felt both curated and natural.

Jusangjeolli Cliff was one of the day's highlights - hexagonal basalt columns rising from the sea, formed by volcanic lava cooling against the ocean. The geometric precision of the rock formations against the crashing waves was mesmerising. Nearby, Cheonjeyeon Waterfall cascaded through lush subtropical forest into emerald pools below.

Lunch at Mannam Garden was a Korean well-being buffet featuring seasonal vegetables and local ingredients - a welcome break before the afternoon. We then visited the Osulloc Tea Museum, set among rolling green tea fields. The museum traced Jeju's tea culture, and we tried fresh green tea made from leaves grown just outside the windows. The fields themselves, perfectly manicured rows of vivid green against the volcanic landscape, were stunning.

The afternoon took us to Songaksan Mountain with its coastal trails and ocean views, then to Bomunsa Temple at Sanbangsan, a cave temple tucked into the side of a volcanic peak with views stretching out to sea. We were dropped off at Dongmun Market around 17:30, tired but with a much deeper sense of Jeju's dramatic landscape.

## East Coast Day Tour

Sunday was the east coast's turn. Another early pickup at 08:25 took us in the opposite direction.

We started at Sunrise Land, then moved on to Seongeup Folk Village - a preserved traditional village where thatched-roof stone houses still stand as they have for centuries. Walking through the village, we learned about Jeju's traditional way of life, from the stone walls that protect against the island's fierce winds to the unique gate system that signals whether residents are home.

Lunch was at Halla Restaurant - sizzling plates of Jeju-style stir-fried black pork. Jeju black pork had been on our list since arriving, and it didn't disappoint. The meat was rich and flavourful, cooked right at the table.

After lunch, we headed to Gwangchigi Beach with its volcanic rock coastline, then watched a haenyeo diving performance. These women free-dive without oxygen tanks to harvest seafood from the ocean floor - a tradition recognised by UNESCO. Watching them work the water with such skill and confidence was humbling. The tradition is dying out as younger generations move to other careers, making seeing it in person feel all the more special.

The day's crown jewel was Seongsan Ilchulbong - the Sunrise Peak. This volcanic crater rising from the sea is one of Jeju's most iconic landmarks. The climb to the rim revealed views of the crater bowl on one side and the ocean stretching endlessly on the other. Even without catching the famous sunrise, the views were breathtaking.

We finished the day cooling off at Hamdeok Beach, one of Jeju's most popular beaches with its turquoise water and white sand - a tropical scene that felt more Southeast Asian than Korean. We were back at the hotel by 17:30.

## Peaks and Temples

Monday was our last full day on Jeju, and we spent it hiking. We started at Sarabong Park, climbing to Sarabong Peak for panoramic views over Jeju City and the coastline. The park was quiet in the morning, locals doing their daily walks along the trails.

From there, we visited Borimsa Temple, a peaceful Buddhist temple tucked into the hillside. The temple grounds were serene - colourful lanterns, stone pagodas, and the sound of chanting drifting through the trees.

The afternoon was for oreum - Jeju's signature parasitic volcanic cones that dot the landscape. We climbed Al Oreum, one of the island's many small volcanic hills, with its grassy slopes and 360-degree views of the countryside. Then we pushed on to Byeoldobong Peak, another striking formation with views back over Jeju City. The oreums gave us a sense of Jeju's volcanic personality - the island is essentially built from these eruptions, hundreds of small cones scattered across the landscape.

## Jeju to Busan

Tuesday morning we checked out and took a taxi to the airport for the 08:45 flight to Busan. Just an hour later, we landed at Gimhae International Airport - another quick domestic hop.
