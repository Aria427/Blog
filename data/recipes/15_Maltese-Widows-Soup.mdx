Soppa tal-Armla, or Widow's Soup, is a beloved classic from Malta's culinary heritage.

This humble yet nourishing soup was traditionally prepared by widows who needed to stretch simple, affordable ingredients into a satisfying meal. Packed with seasonal vegetables, enriched with kunserva (Maltese tomato paste), and topped with poached eggs and ġbejniet (traditional Maltese cheeselets), it's a testament to resourceful home cooking. Every spoonful delivers warmth and comfort - the kind of dish that has sustained generations of Maltese families.

<b>Ingredients</b>:
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1 medium onion, diced
- 2 medium / large potatoes, chopped
- 3 large carrots, chopped
- 1 medium zucchini, chopped
- 1/2 half head of broccoli, chopped
- 300g broad beans
- 100g frozen peas
- 2 tbsp kunserva (traditional Maltese tomato paste)
- 1 litre vegetable stock
- Additions: 
  - 4 large eggs
  - 4 ġbejniet (traditional Maltese cheeselets)

<b>Instructions</b>:
1. In a large pot over medium-heat, add olive oil.
2. Add the diced onion and sauté until it has softened.
3. Add the chopped potatoes, carrots, zucchini and broccoli and sauté for a couple of minutes.
4. Add the broad beans and peas and again sauté for a couple of minutes.
5. Mix in the kunserva.
6. Pour the vegetable stock over everything and wait until it starts to boil.
7. Reduce to a low heat and allow to simmer until all the vegetables are cooked through.
8. Additions: Crack open the eggs directly into the soup and place the ġbejniet on top. Allow to simmer until the eggs are cooked. 
9. Serve warm.

<b>Servings</b>: 4

<b>Nutrition</b> per serving of soup (without the additions):

Ladle into deep bowls and enjoy. The eggs and ġbejniet are optional but add extra richness and protein.
