There’s something about fudge that feels instantly nostalgic and comforting. For my first ever recipe post on Nomad Gourmet Chronicles, I wanted to share a treat that’s both indulgent and health-conscious - a fudge recipe you can enjoy any time.

This healthy fudge is easy to make, packed with wholesome ingredients, and suits an active, mindful lifestyle. Even better, it’s lactose-free, gluten-free, and refined sugar-free.

<b>Ingredients</b>:
- 200g dry lentils (red/yellow/brown - either works)
- 250g dark chocolate 
- 1 tsp gochujang 
- Optional:
  - 1-2 tbsp plant milk (soy/oat/almond - whatever you prefer)
  - 1-8 tsp peanut butter 

<b>Instructions</b>:
1. Line a 8x8 baking dish with baking paper.
1. Cook lentils in 1.5 cups of water (or according to your package's instructions).
2. Melt the chocolate using a bain-marie or in the microwave.
3. Blend the lentils, chocolate and gochujang together until smooth.
4. If the consistency seems too thick, add/blend some plant milk in the mixture as well. 
5. Pour mixture into prepared baking dish and smooth it evenly out.
5. Chill for at least two hours in the fridge.
6. Cut into 8 squares and enjoy.
7. Optional: Spread 1 tsp  of peanut butter on each square before serving. 

<b>Macros</b> per serving based on yellow lentils, 50% dark chocolate, 1 tbsp unsweetened soy milk:
- Protein   8.2g    
- Carb      33g      
- Fat       9.7g     
- Kcal      254

This healthy fudge is proof that you don’t have to compromise on flavor or wellbeing. It’s the perfect pick-me-up after a busy day, a workout treat, or a sweet addition to your travel snack stash. I hope you enjoy making - and sharing - this first recipe as much as I did.
