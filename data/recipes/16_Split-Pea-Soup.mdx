This split pea soup is comfort in its simplest form.

Just a handful of ingredients and a little patience is all it takes. Dried split peas simmer down with potato and onion into a thick, velvety soup that's deeply satisfying. A few sprigs of fresh mint lift the whole thing - it's the kind of no-fuss meal that warms you from the inside out, especially on a cold evening.

<b>Ingredients</b>:
- 200g dried split peas
- 1 large onion, peeled and chopped
- 1 medium-large potato, chopped
- 800ml vegetable stock
- 1 tsp black pepper
- Few sprigs of mint 

<b>Instructions</b>:
1. Wash the split peas.
2. Add the peas, the chopped onion and the chopped potato to a pot.
3. Add the stock and cook until the peas are soft.
4. Mash or liquidise the mix.
5. Season with pepper and mint to taste.

<b>Servings</b>: 4

<b>Nutrition</b> per serving:

| Protein | Carbs | Fat  | Kcal |
| ------- | ----- | ---- | ---- |
| 13g     | 49g   | 1g   | 255  |

---

Ladle into deep bowls and serve hot - maybe grate some cheese on top if you're feeling fancy. Leftovers thicken up overnight and reheat beautifully on the stovetop.
