Brownies are a classic sweet treat, and this version has a nutritious twist - chickpeas add unexpected protein and fiber while keeping them wonderfully fudgy.

Easy to make, packed with wholesome ingredients, and happens to be lactose-free and gluten-free.

<b>Ingredients</b>:
- 1 can of chickpeas; drained and rinsed (around 240g)
- 125g peanut butter
- 50ml maple syrup
- 100ml plant milk (soy/oat/almond - whatever you prefer)
- 1 tbsp coconut oil
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- 25g almond flour
- 30g cocoa powder
- 1/4 tsp baking powder
- 1/4 tsp baking soda
- 1/4 tsp salt

<b>Instructions</b>:
1. Pre-heat the oven to 180C. 
2. Line a small square cake tin with baking paper.
3. Add the chickpeas, peanut butter, milk, coconut oil and vanilla to a blender and blend until smooth.
4. Fold in the almond flour, cocoa powder, baking powder, baking soda and salt.
5. Pour mixture into prepared baking dish and smooth it evenly out.
6. Bake for around 15-20 minutes.
7. Cut into 8 squares and enjoy.

<b>Servings</b>: 8 squares

<b>Macros</b> per serving - based on unsweetened soy milk:
- Protein   7.5g   
- Carb      13.4g     
- Fat       13.1g   
- Kcal      201

Perfect for chocolate lovers looking for a healthier way to satisfy their brownie cravings.
