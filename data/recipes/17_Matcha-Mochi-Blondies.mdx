These matcha mochi blondies are a beautiful marriage of East and West.

The rice flour gives them that signature chewy, mochi-like texture, while white chocolate adds sweetness that balances the earthy matcha perfectly. They're naturally gluten-free and have a unique soft, tender crumb that's completely different from traditional blondies.

<b>Ingredients</b>:
- 90g plant-based butter
- 170g white chocolate
- 1 egg
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- 280ml plant milk (soy/oat/almond - whatever you prefer)
- 150g rice flour
- 6g matcha powder
- 130g caster sugar
- 1 tsp baking powder
- A pinch of salt

<b>Instructions</b>:
1. Preheat the oven to 160°C and line a 20×20 cm (8×8 inch) baking pan with parchment paper.
2. Melt the white chocolate and butter together, stirring until smooth. Set aside.
3. In a bowl, whisk the egg with vanilla extract until combined. Add the milk and mix well.
4. In another bowl, sift together the glutinous rice flour, sugar, matcha powder, baking powder, and salt.
5. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry, stirring until a smooth batter forms.
6. Pour the batter into the prepared baking pan, tapping gently to release air bubbles.
<div className="my-1 w-80 overflow-hidden px-2 xl:my-1 xl:w-1/2 xl:px-2">

7. Bake for about 20 minutes or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.
8. Let cool completely before slicing into squares. Serve and enjoy!
