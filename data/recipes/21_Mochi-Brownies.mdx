These mochi brownies are rich, fudgy, and delightfully chewy thanks to rice flour.

Melting dark chocolate with butter creates a deeply indulgent base, while the rice flour gives them that signature mochi-like texture - soft, dense, and wonderfully chewy. They're naturally gluten-free and lactose-free too. Quick to mix up and perfect for sharing.

<b>Ingredients</b>:
- 75g plant-based butter
- 150g dark chocolate
- 1 egg
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- 280ml plant milk (soy/oat/almond - whatever you prefer)
- 150g rice flour
- 130g demerara sugar
- 1 tsp baking powder
- A pinch of salt

<b>Instructions</b>:
1. Preheat the oven to 160°C and line a 20×20 cm (8×8 inch) baking pan with parchment paper.
2. Melt the dark chocolate and butter together, stirring until smooth. Set aside.
3. In a bowl, whisk the egg with vanilla extract until combined. Add the milk and mix well.
4. In another bowl, sift together the glutinous rice flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
5. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry, stirring until a smooth batter forms.
6. Pour the batter into the prepared baking pan, tapping gently to release air bubbles.
<div className="my-1 w-80 overflow-hidden px-2 xl:my-1 xl:w-1/2 xl:px-2">

7. Bake for about 20 minutes or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.
8. Let cool completely before slicing into squares. Serve and enjoy!
