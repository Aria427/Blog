There’s something timeless about quiche: it’s equally suited for lazy brunches, weeknight dinners, or even a quick packed lunch. This version keeps things light and nourishing by using lactose-free cottage cheese for extra protein and creaminess, and a crisp gluten-free pie crust as the base. 

It’s delicious warm or at room temperature, and offers the flexibility to add seasonal vegetables or extra herbs to fit your mood.

<b>Ingredients</b>:
- 1/2 portion gluten free savoury pie dough (recipe: https://nomad-gourmet-chronicles.vercel.app/recipes/4_Pie-Dough)
- 4 large eggs
- 200g cottage cheese (lactose free)
- 60 ml unsweetened plant milk (soy/oat/almond - whatever you prefer)
- 1/4 tsp salt
- 1/4 tsp pepper
- 2 spring onions, chopped
- 50g parmesan cheese

<b>Instructions</b>:
1. Preheat oven to 180 degrees Celsius.
2. Roll out the pie dough on parchment paper and fit in to a pie dish.
3. Add eggs, cottage cheese, milk, salt and pepper to a medium mixing bowl and whisk until smooth and combined.
4. Add chopped spring onions and grated cheese to the egg mixture and stir to combine.
5. Pour egg mixture into the prepared dish.

6. Place in the oven and bake for 30-45 minutes until set and golden brown around edges. It’s okay if the middle is still a little jiggly, the quiche will still set as it rests.
7. Let the quiche rest at room temperature for 10 minutes, then cut into slices and serve.

<b>Servings</b>: 4

<b>Macros</b> per serving - based on soy milk:
- Protein      18g
- Carb         27g  
- Fat          16g 
- Kcal         325

This cottage cheese quiche is an easy, protein-packed meal with a creamy texture and delicate flavour. Enjoy it fresh from the oven or chilled for later - perfect evidence that wholesome, gluten- and lactose-free food can still feel like a treat.
