Making home-baked pies is one of life’s true comforts, but finding a reliable pastry recipe that’s both gluten-free and lactose-free can feel like a challenge. I've experimented with countless dough recipes and finally landed on this one—my go-to savoury pie dough. It’s flaky and tender, comes together with simple ingredients, and stands up beautifully to all your favourite fillings.

Whether you’re prepping for a hearty family dinner or need a make-ahead dough for meal prep, this recipe offers versatility without sacrificing flavour or texture. Suitable for a variety of savoury pies, it’s become a staple in my kitchen, especially when catering for a range of dietary needs.

<b>Ingredients</b>:
- 960g gluten free plain flour (+ extra for kneading)
- 2 tsp xanthan gum
- 1 packet vegetable margarine (around 500g)
- 400ml water

<b>Instructions</b>:
1. Sift the flour in a bowl.
2. Cut the margarine into cubes and add it to the flour.
3. Mash with your hands until a breadcrumb mixture forms.
4. Spread the mixture to have a hole in the centre.
5. Add all the water in the middle.
6. Mix everything together until the dough forms, ideally using a stand mixer.

7. Take the dough out of the bowl and knead until it has the proper consistency. 
8. Divide dough into 4 separate pieces for full pies. If you plan to use the dough only as a base (example, for a quiche) divide into 8 pieces.

9. Roll out as needed or freeze for later use.

<b>Servings</b>: 4 full pie dough rounds

This pie dough is my trusty base for quiches, savoury tarts, and pies - adaptable for whatever flavour adventure you’re planning next. Enjoy experimenting!
