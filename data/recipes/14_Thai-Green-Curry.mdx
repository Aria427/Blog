This Thai green curry is comfort in a bowl.

Aromatic ginger and garlic meet creamy coconut milk and vibrant green curry paste for a dish that's bursting with flavour. The fish and shrimps add satisfying protein while the vegetables bring colour and freshness. It's the kind of weeknight dinner that feels like a treat - warming, fragrant, and ready faster than delivery.

Note: Feel free to use any mix of vegetables you have on hand - bell peppers, snap peas, baby corn, or broccoli all work beautifully here.

<b>Ingredients</b>:
- 1 small onion, finely chopped
- 2 garlic cloves minced or thinly sliced
- 1 small piece of (peeled) finely chopped ginger
- 1 small zucchini
- 1 medium carrot
- 100g shrimps
- 350g white fish fillets
- 2 tbsp green curry paste, adjust to taste
- 1 tsp ground turmeric, optional
- 1 tbsp fish sauce
- 1 tbsp gluten-free soy sauce
- 400 ml coconut milk 

<b>Instructions</b>:
1. Heat some non-stick spray in a wok and once warm sauté the onions until soft. 
2. Next add the ginger and garlic and cook for another 2-3 minutes. 
3. Add the vegetables and sauté for a few minutes. 
4. Add the shrimps and the fish fillets and sauté for a few more minutes.
4. Next add the curry paste, turmeric, fish sauce, soy sauce and stir to mix all ingredients well. 
5. Allow to cook for 3 minutes then pour in the coconut milk. 
6. Cover and simmer under low heat until vegetables have cooked to desired tenderness.  
7. Serve over steamed jasmine rice.

<b>Servings</b>: 4

Serve hot over steamed jasmine rice and let the fragrant sauce soak in. Leftovers reheat beautifully - the flavours only get better the next day.
