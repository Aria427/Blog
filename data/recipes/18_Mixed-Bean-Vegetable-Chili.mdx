This mixed bean chili is comfort in a bowl.

A rich, smoky blend of spices meets hearty beans and vegetables for a chili that's deeply satisfying. The cocoa powder is the secret ingredient here - it adds a subtle depth and richness without any chocolate flavour. Let the crockpot do the work and you'll have a thick, flavour-packed chili ready in no time.

<b>Ingredients</b>:
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 large onion, chopped
- 2 large carrots, chopped
- 1 celery stalk, chopped
- 2 tsp red chili flakes
- 2 tbsp cocoa powder
- 1 tbsp ground cumin
- 2 tsp dried oregano
- 2 tsp paprika
- 1 tsp cayenne pepper
- 1 tsp salt
- 200g frozen sweetcorn
- 1 can mixed beans, drained and rinsed
- 1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
- 2 tsp brown sugar
- 450ml vegetable stock
- 25g tomato paste
- 300g tomato sauce
- 25g corn flour
- 1 tbsp lemon juice

<b>Instructions</b>:
1. Remove the crockpot lid and set aside. Press Saute and add olive oil to the bottom of the pot. Allow to heat up for just a couple minutes.
2. Add the garlic and onion to the pot. Sauté for 2-3 minutes until the onion has softened.
3. Add the carrots and cook while stirring for 5 minutes.
4. Add the chili flakes, cocoa powder, cumin, oregano, paprika, cayenne and salt. Stir to coat the vegetables in the spices and cook for around 1 minute.
5. Add the sweetcorn, beans, chickpeas, brown sugar, vegetable stock and tomato paste. Stir well to combine and stop the Saute function.
6. Carefully pour the tomato sauce on top of the chili but do not stir in allowing the sauce to sit on top.
7. Cover and lock lid. Turn steam release valve to closed. Press Manual and leave the setting at high pressure but set the cook time for 6 minutes. Press Start/Stop.
8. When cooking cycle ends, allow natural pressure release for 10 minutes. Then flip the valve open to release any remaining pressure.
9. Open lid and press Saute. Add the corn flour and stir until the chili has thickened, for 2-3 minutes.
10. Add the lemon juice and stir well to combine.
11. Serve and enjoy!

<b>Servings</b>: 4

Ladle into deep bowls and serve hot on its own, over rice, or topped with some grated cheese. Leftovers thicken up overnight and taste even better the next day - this one's a great meal prep option.
