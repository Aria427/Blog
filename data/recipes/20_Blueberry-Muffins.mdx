These blueberry banana muffins are soft, fruity, and naturally sweetened with ripe bananas and a touch of maple syrup.

Mashed banana keeps them wonderfully moist, while rolled oats and rice flour give them a hearty texture that's naturally gluten-free. Juicy blueberries and white chocolate chips add bursts of flavour in every bite. Quick to mix up and perfect for meal prep or a grab-and-go snack.

<b>Ingredients</b>:
- 2 ripe bananas (approximately 250g)
- 2 large eggs
- 60ml maple syrup
- 40ml olive oil
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- 100g rolled oats
- 100g rice flour
- 20g ground almonds
- 1 tsp baking powder
- 1/2 tsp xanthan gum
- 100g blueberries
- 50g white chocolate chips

<b>Instructions</b>:
1. Preheat oven to 180°C and grease 2 six-well muffin trays.
2. Mash the bananas with a fork in a mixing bowl.
3. Whisk eggs, add to mixture and mix in.
4. Add in maple syrup, olive oil and vanilla extract and mix until well combined.
5. Add oats and sift in rice flour, ground almonds, baking powder and xanthan gum to the same bowl. Mix until a smooth batter forms.
6. Fold in the blueberries and chocolate chips. 
<div className="my-1 w-80 overflow-hidden px-2 xl:my-1 xl:w-1/2 xl:px-2">
    ![Blueberry Muffins Batter](/static/images/sweet/blueberry-muffins_2.jpg)
</div>

7. Divide evenly into 12 muffin wells. 
<div className="my-1 w-80 overflow-hidden px-2 xl:my-1 xl:w-1/2 xl:px-2">
    ![Blueberry Muffins Raw](/static/images/sweet/blueberry-muffins_3.jpg)
</div>

8. Bake for 15 to 20 minutes, until golden and set through the centre.
<div className="my-1 w-80 overflow-hidden px-2 xl:my-1 xl:w-1/2 xl:px-2">
    ![Blueberry Muffins Finished](/static/images/sweet/blueberry-muffins_4.jpg)
</div>

9. Let cool completely before scooping out. Serve and enjoy!
