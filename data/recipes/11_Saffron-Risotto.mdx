Golden, creamy, and utterly luxurious - this saffron risotto is restaurant-quality comfort food made easy.

The delicate floral notes of saffron transform simple arborio rice into something truly special. The crockpot does all the work for you, slowly coaxing out that silky, creamy texture without the constant stirring. This is the kind of dish that makes you feel fancy, even on a Tuesday night - with minimal effort required.

<b>Ingredients</b>:
- 8g vegan butter
- 1 medium onion, finely chopped
- 200g Arborio rice
- 450ml vegetable stock
- 50ml white wine
- 1 tsp lemon juice
- 1 pinch saffron
- 50g parmesan cheese

<b>Instructions</b>:
1. Remove the crockpot lid and set aside. Press Saute and add butter to the bottom of the pot. Allow to heat up for just a couple minutes.
2. Add the onion to the pot. Sauté for 5 minutes until the onion has softened and started to brown.
3. Add rice and cook while stirring for 5 minutes.
4. Grind the saffron with a mortar and pestle.
5. Pour the saffron into the pot, then put the wine in the mortar and pour it into the pot as well.
6. Stir and cook for 1 minute until the wine has been absorbed.
7. Add the broth and lemon juice, stir and stop the Saute function.
8. Cover and lock lid. Turn steam release valve to closed. Press Rice/Risotto and leave the time set for 12 minutes. Press Start/Stop.
9. When cooking cycle ends, allow natural pressure release for 5 minutes. Then flip the valve open to release any remaining pressure.
10. Open lid and stir in the parmesan cheese. 
11. Serve and enjoy!

<b>Servings</b>: 2
